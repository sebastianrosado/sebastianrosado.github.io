Intro

I'm passionate about machine learning, data visualization and dashboarding. It's exciting to mine massive datasets for insights, and crucial to be able to communicate your findings clearly and convincingly to the rest of your team. Check out my work.

Work

Properly segmenting hotel guests allows us to understand their needs better. In this project, I explored segmentation by country of origin. Do people of different nationalities rate the same hotels differently? To answer the question, I analyze reviews for the Hilton Hotels and develop a dashboard where you can explore the data.
How does your country of origin affect your hotel experience?

How do you broaden financial inclusion for people with scarce or non-existing credit histories? One solution is to leverage large numbers of unconventional datapoints to predict a client's ability to pay off their loans on time. In this project, I use three machine learning algorithms to identify whether a client will be able to pay back their loans on time, as well as the variables most associated with these outcomes.
Banking the Unbanked: Predicting Risk for Consumer Loans

Twitter is what people are talking about. Around 500 million tweets are sent each day, meaning 6000 a second. I began this project in the wake of George Floyd's death, and decided to focus this project on him as a small tribute. I ran unsupervised learning techniques on around 10,000 unique tweets I scraped myself, clustering them into distinct conversations being had on the platform.
What conversations are people having online around George Floyd?

Even though modern remotes and keyboards date from the 1980s, they continue to be our primary forms of interaction with TVs and computers. In the age of IOT, we should simply not be anchored to hardware to interface with our media. This project is was an experiment in increasing human-computer bandwidth with neural networks and under-utilized input devices.
Real time gesture recognition with Python & OpenCV | Demo
